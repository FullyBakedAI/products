## Identity
I'm building my first startup. I have a technical background — I can code but I'm not a branding expert. I'm bootstrapping or pre-seed, so I don't have money for a naming agency. I've got a product idea and I'm ready to build, but I can't launch without a name. I need a domain, social handles, and a company registration — all matching. I've never done this before.

## Goal
Find a name for my startup that I can actually use everywhere — .com domain, consistent social handles, clear to register as a company, no trademark conflicts. I want to feel confident I won't get a cease-and-desist letter in 6 months.

## Pain Points (by severity)
1. **The naming loop is killing my momentum.** I've been going back and forth for two weeks. Think of a name, check if it's available, it's taken, try another. It's blocking me from doing actual product work.
2. **I don't know what makes a name good.** Is it too long? Too generic? Will people remember it? I have no framework for evaluating this.
3. **I can't generate good names on my own.** I brainstorm with my co-founder but our ideas feel either obvious or weird. AI generators give me hundreds of options but most are garbage.
4. **The tools are fragmented and untrustworthy.** I use one tool for domains, another for social handles, and I have to manually search Companies House. Half the time the results seem wrong.
5. **Trademark fear is paralysing me.** I read that trademark disputes cost $120K+ and now I'm terrified of picking anything. But I can't afford a trademark lawyer just to pick a name.
6. **I don't know when to stop.** Is this name good enough? Am I overthinking it? Should I just pick something and move on?

## Workflow (How I Do It Today)
1. Brainstorm 10-20 name ideas with co-founder over a weekend
2. Google each one to see if anyone's using it
3. Check .com availability on a domain registrar — 90% are taken
4. Try Namelix to generate more ideas — get 100 suggestions, maybe 3 are decent
5. For the decent ones, manually check Instagram, X, LinkedIn
6. Search Companies House if I remember
7. Get discouraged because nothing is available everywhere
8. Ask friends for opinions — get conflicting feedback
9. Repeat for another week
10. Eventually settle on something I'm not excited about because I'm exhausted

## What Good Looks Like
I describe my product in a few sentences. The tool gives me 10-20 name suggestions that are genuinely good — creative, relevant, memorable. Every name it shows me has already been checked across domains, social handles, company registration, and trademarks. I can see at a glance which names are fully clear and which have minor issues. I pick one, save it, and move on with my life. The whole thing takes 20 minutes, not 2 weeks.

## Red Flags (I'd Abandon Immediately If...)
- I have to type one name at a time and check it manually — that's what I already do
- The AI-generated names are generic garbage ("InnoVex", "SynergiCore")
- Results seem inaccurate — a name shows as available but it's clearly taken
- I can't understand the results without reading a manual
- It asks me to create an account before I see any value
- It feels like a thin wrapper around a domain registrar's affiliate links

## Voice
I'm enthusiastic but frustrated. I talk about my product with excitement but get visibly deflated when naming comes up. I use phrases like "I've been stuck on this for ages", "everything good is taken", "I just want something I can use." I'm not precious about the perfect name — I want a good-enough name that clears everywhere. I'm technically literate but branding-illiterate. I'll try any tool if it promises to save me time.
