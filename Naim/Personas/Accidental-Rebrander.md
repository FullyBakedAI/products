## Identity
I'm 6 months to 2 years into building my business. I chose my name quickly at the start — checked the domain was available, maybe glanced at Instagram, registered the company, and moved on. Now I'm facing consequences: a cease-and-desist letter, a trademark dispute, or I've discovered someone with an identical company name in my sector. I need to rebrand, and I need to do it right this time. I'm terrified of making the same mistake twice.

## Goal
Find a new name with absolute certainty that it's clear everywhere — domains, social handles, company registration, trademarks, app stores. I cannot afford another naming disaster. I need comprehensive clearance and I need it fast because every day on the old name increases my legal risk.

## Pain Points (by severity)
1. **I got burned because I didn't check properly the first time.** I didn't know I needed to check trademarks. Or I checked domains but not company registration. Now I'm paying the price — legal fees, rebranding costs, lost brand equity.
2. **Rebranding is horrifyingly expensive.** New domain, new social handles, updated legal filings, reprinted materials, SEO impact, customer confusion. I'm looking at $50K+ in direct costs and months of disruption.
3. **I'm under time pressure.** The cease-and-desist gives me 30-60 days. I can't spend weeks in a naming loop. I need a name that's definitively clear and I need it now.
4. **I don't trust my own judgement anymore.** I thought I'd done enough checking last time. Clearly I hadn't. I need external validation that a name is safe.
5. **Trademark complexity terrifies me.** I now understand that trademark law is category-specific, phonetically-based, and subjective. I don't have the expertise to evaluate this myself but I also can't afford $500/hour trademark attorneys for an extended naming process.

## Workflow (How I Do It Today)
1. Receive cease-and-desist or discover the naming conflict
2. Panic. Consult a lawyer. Learn how expensive this is going to be.
3. Need a new name immediately
4. This time, hire a naming agency ($15K-50K) OR try to do it myself with extreme thoroughness
5. If doing it myself: generate ideas, then obsessively check every database I can find
6. Check domains, social handles, Companies House, TESS, WIPO, EUIPO — manually, one by one
7. Still not confident. Did I miss something? Is there a database I don't know about?
8. Eventually commit to a name but with lingering anxiety
9. Register everything as fast as possible before something changes

## What Good Looks Like
I enter a name and get a comprehensive, definitive report: every domain TLD, every social platform, every company register, every trademark database. Clear confidence levels — "confirmed clear" vs "couldn't verify." A risk assessment I can show my lawyer. The ability to generate alternatives that are ALL pre-verified if my first choice has issues. And a PDF report I can file as evidence of due diligence.

## Red Flags (I'd Abandon Immediately If...)
- Results feel superficial — I need depth, not breadth of platforms nobody uses
- No trademark checking — that's the whole reason I'm here
- Can't distinguish between "we checked and it's clear" and "we didn't check" — I need that confidence model
- Accuracy is questionable — I cannot be burned twice
- No exportable report — I need documentation for legal purposes
- Feels casual or lightweight — this is a serious business situation, not a fun branding exercise

## Voice
Stressed, thorough, risk-averse. I've learned lessons the hard way and I'm not going to cut corners again. I say things like "I need to be absolutely sure this time", "what databases are you checking?", "can I see exactly what was verified?", "I need this for my lawyer." I'm willing to pay significant money for certainty — $50 for a comprehensive report is nothing compared to $50K in rebranding costs. I need the tool to feel professional and authoritative, not playful.
