## 1. Core Problem Definition

### The Problem
It's not about finding a name. It's about finding a name you can **actually own everywhere**.

Founders, creators, and businesses don't just need a name — they need a name that works as a complete brand identity: available domain (ideally .com), consistent social handles across every platform that matters, available as a registered company name, and clear of trademark conflicts. Today, verifying all of this is a fragmented, manual, unreliable process that can take weeks.

The real problem is **brand identity availability** — not just name checking.

### Who Has This Problem
- **Startup founders** — can't incorporate, launch a website, or build a social presence until the name clears everywhere
- **Creators and solopreneurs** — personal brand consistency across platforms is their business
- **Agencies and brand consultants** — do this for clients repeatedly at scale, currently stitching together 5+ tools
- **Product teams** — launching new products, features, or sub-brands within existing companies
- **Domain investors** — evaluating whether a name has full-stack brand potential, not just domain value

### The Full Stack of Brand Identity
A name isn't ready until it clears **all** of these:

| Layer | What It Means | Why It Matters |
|-------|--------------|----------------|
| **Domain availability** | .com, .io, .co, .ai, country codes | Your web presence — .com is still king for credibility |
| **Social handle availability** | Instagram, TikTok, X, YouTube, LinkedIn, GitHub, etc. | Brand consistency — @yourname everywhere or it's fragmented |
| **Company name registration** | Companies House (UK), state registries (US), etc. | Legal requirement to incorporate — can't trade without it |
| **Trademark clearance** | USPTO, EUIPO, WIPO databases | Legal risk — someone else owning the mark in your category can shut you down |
| **App store availability** | iOS App Store, Google Play | If you're building an app, the name needs to be clear here too |

No existing tool checks all five layers. Most check one or two. The result is that people commit to a name based on partial information and discover conflicts later — sometimes after they've already built a brand around it.

### The Pain
1. **Fragmented workflow** — users bounce between domain registrars, individual social platforms, Companies House, trademark databases, and app stores. Five+ separate tools, five+ separate searches.
2. **Inaccurate results** — existing checkers frequently show false positives/negatives; users can't trust the results
3. **No intelligence** — current tools check exact matches only. They don't tell you "this is taken but here are strong alternatives" or "this name has trademark risk in your category"
4. **The naming loop** — founders get stuck in a cycle: think of name → check → partially taken → think of another → check → partially taken. This loop can last weeks and is demoralising
5. **Partial availability is useless** — a name available as a .com but taken on Instagram is a brand consistency problem from day one. You need the full picture before you commit.
6. **Late discovery of conflicts** — finding out after incorporation that your company name has a trademark conflict in your category, or that the social handles are all squatted. Expensive to unwind.

### What Good Looks Like
One search. Full picture. Every layer of brand identity checked simultaneously — domains, social handles, company registration, trademarks, app stores. A clear signal: **"This name is yours to take"** or **"Here's what's blocking you and here are alternatives that clear everywhere."**

## 3. Competitive Landscape (Deep Research)

### Tier 1: Pure Name Checkers (Check Only)

| Tool | What It Does | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|------|-------------|-----------|------------|
| **Namechk** | Checks 36 domain extensions + 100+ social platforms | Broadest platform coverage, well-known brand | Inaccurate results reported by users, CAPTCHA friction, no intelligence layer, dated UI |
| **Namecheckr** | Domain + social handle checking | Clean UI, fast | Limited platforms compared to Namechk, no suggestions |
| **Namecheck.com** | Similar multi-platform checking | Simple interface | Limited differentiation, commodity feature set |
| **BrandSnag** | Domains, social handles, gaming usernames | Includes ENS (Ethereum) domain checking, business name generator | Premium tier pricing unclear, limited AI |
| **Namecheckly** | Domain + social media availability | Unlimited free searches, smart name suggestions | New entrant, unclear revenue model |
| **CheckUser.org** | Username + domain across 70+ sites | Decent coverage | Basic, no AI, no suggestions |
| **Instant Username Search** | Real-time username checking | Open source, fast, clean | Domain checking only, no trademark |

**Market dynamics:** This tier is crowded, commoditized, and differentiation is minimal. Most compete on platform count. Revenue comes from affiliate links to registrars — a thin margin play. User trust is low due to inaccuracy.

### Tier 2: AI Name Generators (Generate + Check)

| Tool | What It Does | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|------|-------------|-----------|------------|
| **Namelix** | AI-generated brandable names + domain check | Learning algorithm, short branded names, free | No social handle checking, no trademark, names can feel generic |
| **Looka** | Name generation + logo design + domain check | Full brand identity package | Name generation is secondary to logo, expensive for naming alone |
| **Namify** | AI name generation + domain + logo | Creative names with visuals | Limited platform checking |
| **Shopify Name Generator** | Keyword-based name generation + domain check | Free, large user base | Basic generation, limited to Shopify ecosystem |
| **Grammarly Business Name Generator** | AI-powered name suggestions | Trusted brand, quality output | Limited to naming, no availability checking beyond domains |

**Market dynamics:** This tier is growing fast. AI generation is the hook but availability checking is shallow — usually just domains, rarely social handles, never trademarks. The generation is often keyword-stuffing, not genuinely creative.

### Tier 3: AI Trademark Search (Legal Layer)

| Tool | What It Does | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|------|-------------|-----------|------------|
| **Trademarkia.ai** | AI-powered trademark search + filing | Market leader, comprehensive database | Expensive, complex UI, not for casual users |
| **Huski.ai** | AI trademark search + monitoring | Advanced AI, brand protection | Enterprise pricing, overkill for name checking |
| **TrademarkNow (Corsearch)** | AI clearance + watch | Reduces clearance time by 40%, NLP + visual similarity | Enterprise only, $$$, not consumer-facing |

**Market dynamics:** Trademark tools are powerful but completely siloed from the naming/domain/social checking workflow. No one connects all three layers.

### The Gap
**Nobody checks the full stack of brand identity.** Today a founder must:
1. Think of a name
2. Check domains on a name checker (Tier 1)
3. Check social handles individually or on a different tool
4. Check company name availability on Companies House / state registries
5. Search trademarks on USPTO or Trademarkia (Tier 3)
6. Check app store availability manually
7. If **any one** of these fails, go back to step 1
8. Optionally use an AI generator (Tier 2) for ideas, then repeat steps 2-7

This is a broken, fragmented workflow. Nobody checks all five layers (domains, social, company registration, trademarks, app stores) in one place. And none of these tools use AI to actually help you *navigate* the problem — they just check yes/no on a subset of what matters.

## 4. AI-Augmented Differentiation

### What's Been Done (and Where AI Is Just a Checkbox)
- AI name *generation* exists (Namelix, Looka, Shopify) — but it's mostly keyword recombination with ML flavor
- AI trademark *search* exists (Trademarkia, Corsearch) — but it's enterprise-priced and siloed
- No tool uses AI for the *decision-making layer* between checking and acting

### What AI Could Genuinely Unlock for Naim

#### 1. Intelligent Name Scoring
Not just "available/taken" but a **composite score** that weighs:
- Domain availability (and quality — .com vs .io vs .xyz)
- Social handle consistency (how many platforms match?)
- Trademark risk (phonetic similarity, category overlap)
- Brand strength signals (pronounceability, memorability, length, linguistic analysis)
- Cultural/linguistic risk (does it mean something bad in another language?)

**This doesn't exist anywhere.** Every tool gives you a binary check. Naim could give you a score and explain *why*.

#### 2. AI-Powered Alternatives Engine
When a name is taken, don't just say "taken." Use AI to:
- Suggest phonetic variations that preserve the brand feel
- Find available alternatives that keep the semantic meaning
- Propose creative modifications (prefixes, suffixes, portmanteaus)
- Rank alternatives by the same composite score

**The naming loop is the core pain point.** AI can break it.

#### 3. Brand Context Understanding
Let the user describe their business/product in natural language. AI understands the domain and generates names that are contextually relevant — not just keyword mashups. "I'm building a sustainable pet food delivery service" should yield fundamentally different suggestions than "I'm launching a fintech API."

#### 4. Trademark Risk Assessment (Lightweight)
Use free USPTO/WIPO databases + AI phonetic analysis to flag potential trademark conflicts — without needing a $500/search enterprise tool. Not legal advice, but an early warning system that saves users from investing in a name that's going to get a cease-and-desist.

### The Hook
**"One name. Every channel. Full picture."** — Naim doesn't just check if a name is available on one platform. It checks the full stack of brand identity — domains, social handles, company registration, trademarks, app stores — and tells you whether this name is truly yours to take. And when it's not, AI finds you one that is.

## 5. New Opportunities

### Things Nobody's Doing Yet

#### 1. Name Portfolio Management
Creators and businesses don't just check one name once. They build and manage a portfolio of potential names over time. No tool offers a saved workspace where you can compare, score, and track availability changes for a shortlist.

#### 2. Team/Collaborative Naming
Brand naming in agencies and product teams is collaborative. Nobody offers shared workspaces where teams can propose, vote, discuss, and evaluate names together.

#### 3. Availability Monitoring
"This name is taken now, but alert me if the domain expires or the social handle becomes available." Domain expiry monitoring exists as a separate tool — nobody integrates it into the naming workflow.

#### 4. Name-to-Launch Pipeline
The gap between "I found a name" and "I own it everywhere" is entirely manual. Naim could guide users through: register domain → claim social handles → file trademark — as a managed workflow, not just links out to other services.

#### 5. Industry/Niche Intelligence
What naming patterns work in your industry? What TLDs are trending for your sector? What social platforms matter most for your audience? This contextual intelligence doesn't exist in any name checker.

#### 6. White-Label for Agencies
Brand agencies do naming work constantly. A white-label version of Naim that agencies can embed in their client workflows — with their branding — is a direct B2B opportunity. This aligns perfectly with the BakeKit white-label architecture.

The affiliate model means revenue starts from the first user who checks a name and then registers a domain through our referral. No need to wait for a paid user base.

## Go/No-Go Assessment

### GO — with high confidence

**Why:**
1. **The market is large and growing** — $10B+ domain market, 378M+ registrations, 5M+ new businesses annually in the US alone
2. **The competition is weak** — commoditized Tier 1 checkers with accuracy problems, siloed AI generators, enterprise-only trademark tools. Nobody connects the full workflow.
3. **The AI differentiation is real** — name scoring, intelligent alternatives, and lightweight trademark risk are genuinely new capabilities that solve the core pain (the naming loop)
4. **Monetization is immediate** — affiliate revenue from day one, clear upgrade path to Pro/Team/Agency
5. **White-label is a multiplier** — agencies are a natural B2B channel, and this aligns perfectly with BakeKit's architecture
6. **It's a perfect BakedUX proof point** — demonstrates the full methodology from Discovery through to product, documents every step as sellable IP

### Risks to Watch
- **AI cost management** — scoring and suggestions use LLM calls; must keep costs below revenue per user
- **Accuracy is table stakes** — if our results aren't trustworthy, nothing else matters
- **API rate limits** — free social media checking relies on platform tolerance of automated checks
- **Trademark is legally sensitive** — must be very clear that AI trademark flagging is not legal advice
