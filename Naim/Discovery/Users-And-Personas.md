# Naim — Target Markets, Users, and Personas

**Date:** 2026-04-02
**Phase:** Discovery (deepening)
**Purpose:** Understand who we're building for, how they experience the naming problem, and what would make them pay

---

## Target Markets

### Market 1: New Venture Creation
People starting something — a company, a product, a brand. This is the highest-urgency market because naming is a blocker. You can't incorporate, register a domain, claim social handles, or start marketing until you have a name.

**Size:** ~5M new business registrations annually in the US. ~3M in the EU. Creator economy: 50M+ globally.

### Market 2: Brand and Naming Services
Agencies and consultancies who do naming work for clients. This is the highest-value market because they do this repeatedly and will pay for tools that make their workflow faster and more professional.

**Size:** ~120K branding/marketing agencies globally. Naming-specific agencies charge $15K-75K+ per engagement.

### Market 3: Rebrand and Rename
Existing businesses that need to change their name — due to trademark disputes, pivots, mergers, or growth. Less frequent but very high urgency when it happens.

**Size:** Hard to quantify but significant. ~30% of startups rebrand within their first 3 years.

## Personas

### Persona 1: "The First-Time Founder"

**Who they are:**
Solo founder or small co-founding team. Building their first startup. Technical or product background — not branding experts. Usually bootstrapped or pre-seed.

**Context:**
They've got an idea and they're ready to build. But they can't launch without a name. They need a domain, social handles, and ideally a company registration — all matching.

**Their naming process today:**
1. Brainstorm alone or with co-founder (narrow pool of ideas)
2. Google "business name generator" and get overwhelmed with tools
3. Use Namelix or similar to generate ideas — most feel generic
4. Check .com availability for each name manually
5. Find it's taken. Try another. Taken. Another. Taken.
6. Maybe check Instagram, X manually for the few names that have a .com
7. Get stuck in the naming loop for days or weeks
8. Eventually settle on something imperfect out of frustration
9. Discover trademark issues or missing social handles weeks later

**Pain points:**
- **The naming loop is demoralising** — "I've been thinking of a name for weeks now and it's blocking me from doing other tasks"
- **No naming experience** — they don't know what makes a name good or bad
- **Tools generate quantity, not quality** — "Every 1/10 there's an ok one" (on Namelix)
- **Fragmented workflow** — domain checker here, social checker there, Companies House somewhere else
- **Fear of getting it wrong** — trademark litigation costs $120K-750K average. That fear is paralysing.
- **Perfectionism trap** — they want the perfect name but don't know when "good enough" is good enough

**What they'd pay for:**
A tool that generates good names tailored to their idea AND tells them which ones they can actually use everywhere. Breaks the loop. Removes the fear.

**Quote (from research):**
> "I had no idea how hard it is to come up with a name for a product. It's been two weeks and it's blocking me from doing quite a bit of tasks."

### Persona 2: "The Serial Builder"

**Who they are:**
Experienced founder or indie hacker. Has launched multiple products. Technical. Pragmatic about naming — they know it matters but don't want to spend weeks on it.

**Context:**
They're launching their 3rd or 5th product. They know the naming process is painful because they've done it before. They want to move fast. For them, naming is a necessary step to clear, not a creative exercise to enjoy.

**Their naming process today:**
1. Have a rough name idea based on what the product does
2. Immediately check .com availability — if taken, move on
3. Maybe use Namechk to bulk-check social handles
4. Accept imperfection — will use .io or .co if .com is taken
5. Pick something in a few hours, not weeks
6. Might rename later if the product gets traction

**Pain points:**
- **Speed, not creativity** — they want this done in minutes, not days
- **Accuracy of checkers** — "Namechk wasn't accurate in its results" (user review)
- **Repeat workflow** — doing the same manual checking process for every new project
- **The .com scarcity problem** — good .com domains are virtually impossible to find. This person has accepted .io/.co but still wants to know the full picture fast.

**What they'd pay for:**
Speed and accuracy. Give me 20 available names for my product in 60 seconds. Let me pick one and move on. Don't waste my time with names that are taken.

**Quote (from research):**
> "Don't overthink it. Name it whatever the value it brings. Just get the thing launched."

### Persona 3: "The Creator"

**Who they are:**
Content creator, influencer, or personal brand. Active on Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, X. Their username IS their brand.

**Context:**
They need a consistent handle across all platforms. Unlike founders, they don't care about domain names or company registration (at first). Social handle consistency is everything — if followers can't find them with the same @handle on every platform, it fragments their audience.

**Their naming process today:**
1. Think of a handle that represents them
2. Check Instagram — taken by an inactive account from 2017
3. Check TikTok — available
4. Check YouTube — taken by someone with 12 subscribers
5. Try variations: add numbers, underscores, "the", "official"
6. End up with @realSamDesigns on one platform, @samdesigns_ on another, @SamDesignsHQ on a third
7. Brand fragmentation from day one

**Pain points:**
- **Inactive accounts squatting handles** — the biggest frustration. The name is "taken" by someone who posted once in 2019 and disappeared. Can't claim it without a trademark.
- **Platform inconsistency** — different character rules, different availability. What works on X doesn't work on TikTok.
- **The patchwork username problem** — adding underscores and numbers "screams placeholder, not professional"
- **Discovery and growth impact** — inconsistent handles mean "missed follows, missed leads, lost trust"
- **No way to monitor** — if that inactive account gets deleted, they'll never know

**What they'd pay for:**
A tool that finds handles available across ALL their key platforms simultaneously. Bonus: monitors inactive accounts holding their desired handle and alerts when they become available.

**Quote (from research):**
> "A digital designer who has to juggle four separate names across platforms because their preferred handle is never available — it damages brand recognition and trust."

### Persona 4: "The Agency Namer"

**Who they are:**
Brand strategist or naming specialist at a branding agency. Does naming work professionally for clients. Charges $15K-75K per naming engagement.

**Context:**
Naming is their core deliverable. A typical engagement takes 4-6 weeks: strategy/discovery, name generation, trademark screening, domain screening, presentation, final selection. They generate hundreds of candidate names per project and screen them down to 6-10 finalists.

**Their naming process today:**
1. **Week 1:** Client discovery — understand the brand, market, audience
2. **Week 2:** Generate 200+ candidate names (brainstorms, creative sessions)
3. **Week 3:** Screen each name: trademark search (TESS/WIPO), domain availability, social handles — done individually for each name, often by a junior team member
4. **Week 4:** Present 6-10 finalists with brand stories and rationale
5. **Weeks 5-6:** Client selects, agency secures domain and handles

**Pain points:**
- **Screening is manual, tedious, and expensive** — checking 200 names across domains, social, trademark, company registration is hours of junior team time
- **Inaccurate tools** — they've been burned by Namechk showing something as available that wasn't
- **No single tool for full screening** — they stitch together 5+ different tools and databases
- **Client presentation** — there's no professional way to show clients the full availability picture. They make custom slide decks or spreadsheets for each engagement.
- **White-label need** — they don't want to show clients a third-party tool. It undermines their perceived expertise.

**What they'd pay for:**
A professional screening tool that checks names across all channels in bulk. White-labelled with their agency branding. Exports to a polished client-ready report. Saves hours of junior team time per engagement — easily worth $100-300/month against the $15K+ they charge per project.

**Quote (from research):**
> "The standard naming process takes 4-6 weeks. Week 3 — trademark and domain screening — is the most tedious and error-prone part."

### Persona 5: "The Accidental Rebrander"

**Who they are:**
Founder or business owner who chose a name without proper due diligence and is now facing consequences — a trademark dispute, a domain squatter demanding $10K, social handles taken by competitors.

**Context:**
They're 6 months to 2 years into building on a name that has problems. Rebranding now is painful and expensive — they've built brand equity, SEO rankings, printed materials, legal registrations around this name. But continuing is riskier.

**Their process today:**
1. Receive cease-and-desist or trademark notice
2. Panic
3. Need a new name FAST that clears everywhere
4. Even more terrified of getting it wrong a second time
5. May hire a naming agency ($15K+) or try to do it themselves under pressure

**Pain points:**
- **Urgency** — this is a crisis, not a fun creative exercise
- **Fear** — they got burned once. They need certainty this time.
- **Cost of getting it wrong again** — rebranding costs (new domain, new social handles, updated legal filings, reprinted materials, SEO impact) can run $50K+
- **Trademark complexity** — they now understand how painful trademark disputes are and want comprehensive clearance before committing

**What they'd pay for:**
Comprehensive name clearance they can trust. They'd pay premium for the confidence that a name is truly clear across everything — domains, social, trademark, company registration. The Brand Readiness Report is literally what they need.

**Quote (from research):**
> "Pattern89 and Canopy both faced trademark disputes that, while subjective, proved expensive to fight."

## Pain Point Summary (Across All Personas)

| Pain Point | Personas Affected | Severity |
|-----------|------------------|----------|
| **The naming loop** — stuck for days/weeks generating and checking | Founder, Creator | Critical — blocks launch |
| **Fragmented tools** — 5+ separate tools to check everything | All | High — time waste, error-prone |
| **Inaccurate results** — can't trust existing checkers | All | High — erodes confidence |
| **Name generation is weak** — tools produce quantity, not quality | Founder, Serial Builder | High — the starting point is broken |
| **No full-stack view** — nobody checks domains + social + company + trademark + app stores together | All | High — partial information leads to bad decisions |
| **Inactive account squatting** — desired handles taken by dead accounts | Creator | High — biggest creator frustration |
| **Trademark fear** — $120K-750K litigation cost creates paralysis | Founder, Rebrander | Critical — existential risk |
| **No professional output** — can't share or present findings professionally | Agency, Founder (with co-founders/investors) | Medium — workflow gap |
| **Repeat workflow** — doing the same manual process for every new venture | Serial Builder, Agency | Medium — scalability problem |
| **Rebranding cost** — discovering problems after committing to a name | Rebrander | Critical — $50K+ to fix |

## Who to Build For First?

### Primary: The First-Time Founder
- Highest volume (millions per year)
- Highest pain (the naming loop is genuinely blocking them)
- Most likely to discover Naim through search ("business name generator", "domain name checker")
- Free tier converts them, AI features retain them

### Secondary: The Creator
- High volume (50M+ creator economy)
- Distinct pain (social handle consistency, not domains)
- Different entry point but same core product
- Social handle checking is the hook for this persona

### Tertiary (but highest value): The Agency
- Low volume but high willingness to pay
- White-label is the unlock
- They bring their own clients — indirect reach multiplier
- Build for founders first, add agency features later

### Future: The Rebrander
- Low volume, highest urgency and willingness to pay premium
- Needs the most comprehensive checking (trademark especially)
- Good persona for premium/enterprise tier later
