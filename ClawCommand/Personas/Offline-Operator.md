## Identity

I work from everywhere — trains, planes, client sites with terrible WiFi, coffee shops where the connection drops every ten minutes. I've learned not to trust connectivity. My best thinking happens in transit, and I've lost too many ideas to tools that need a live connection to do anything useful. I use a MacBook and iPhone, I value offline-first apps, and I've been burned enough to test what happens when I pull the ethernet cable before I commit to any tool.

## Goal

I want to capture ideas, queue commands, and review previous agent conversations without needing a connection. When I'm back online, everything syncs seamlessly — nothing lost, nothing duplicated, no conflicts.

## Pain Points (by severity)

1. **Ideas evaporate without capture** — I had the breakthrough on the train and by the time I got to the office, it was gone. Quick capture that works offline is non-negotiable.
2. **Tools die without WiFi** — most AI interfaces show a spinner forever or an error page when connection drops. I need graceful degradation, not a dead screen.
3. **Sync conflicts destroy trust** — I've used tools that created duplicates or overwrote my offline work when reconnecting. Once that happens, I never trust the tool again.
4. **Can't review history offline** — I want to read back previous agent conversations while I'm disconnected. The data is already on my device — let me access it.

## Workflow (How I Do It Today)

1. Board a train, open laptop, realise WiFi is unusable
2. Open Notes app and dump thoughts as plain text — no structure, no connection to my AI workflow
3. Land at destination, open actual AI tools, manually re-enter everything from notes
4. Realise I forgot half the context I had on the train
5. Repeat weekly

## What Good Looks Like

I'm on a flight with no WiFi. I open ClawCommand, capture three ideas with quick-capture, review yesterday's agent conversations from local cache, and queue two commands for when I land. The command bar shows a clear "offline" indicator — no pretending. When I land and WiFi connects, a subtle sync indicator shows everything flowing through. No duplicates. No conflicts. No lost work.

## Red Flags (I'd Abandon Immediately If...)

- App shows a blank screen or error without connection
- Offline work is lost or overwritten on sync
- No clear indicator of online/offline state
- Quick capture requires a network round-trip
- Can't access any previous data while disconnected
- Sync creates duplicate entries

## Voice

"Does it work on a plane?" Tests every feature by disconnecting first. Talks about "trust" a lot — trusting the sync, trusting the cache, trusting that nothing is lost. Says "I'll believe it when I see it reconnect cleanly." Would describe the ideal tool as "reliable."
