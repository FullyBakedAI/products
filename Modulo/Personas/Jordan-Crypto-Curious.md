## Identity

I'm Jordan. 34. Work in marketing. I bought £2,000 of Bitcoin and £1,500 of Ethereum on Coinbase two years ago, mostly because I kept seeing it in the news and felt like I was missing out. It's sitting there. I check the price too often. I know I should be "doing something" with it — I've heard people talk about staking and earning yield — but every time I look into it I end up in a rabbit hole of jargon: gas fees, wallets, seed phrases, bridging. I closed the tab three times. I still haven't moved my crypto off Coinbase.

I'm not stupid. I understand compound interest. I use apps well. But this space feels deliberately hostile to newcomers, and I'm not sure I trust myself not to lose everything by clicking the wrong thing.

## Goal

Get my crypto working for me without becoming a crypto expert. I want something that feels as safe and simple as a savings account, but gives me better returns. I need to be able to trust the product before I'll touch my coins.

## Pain Points

1. **Jargon kills confidence** — "cross-chain collateralisation" means nothing to me. Neither does "non-custodial." These terms make me feel excluded.
2. **Fear of irreversible mistakes** — crypto transactions feel permanent and unforgiving. One wrong click and it's gone. There's no "cancel" button, no customer support number.
3. **Too many steps to do anything** — I've looked at MetaMask. The number of steps to go from BTC on Coinbase to "earning yield somewhere" is terrifying.
4. **Trust signals are missing** — the DeFi space is full of scams. I need to feel like this company is real, regulated, and will be here next year.
5. **I don't know what I'm getting into** — fees, rates, lock-up periods. I need to understand the deal before I commit.

## What Good Looks Like

An app that meets me where I am: shows me what I have, explains clearly what I could do with it, tells me what it will cost, and doesn't make me feel like an idiot. If I can connect my Coinbase account and see "here's your £3,500, here's what you could earn" — I'm interested.

## Red Flags (I'd Leave Immediately If...)

- Wallet seed phrases on first screen
- "Bridge your assets to get started"
- Rates shown without context (5% of what? compared to what?)
- Anything that looks like it was designed for traders, not for me
- No obvious way to contact support

## Voice

"I just want this to be like a better savings account. Can't it just... do it for me?"
