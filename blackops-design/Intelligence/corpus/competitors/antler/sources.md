## Primary facts (from structured searches)

### Model
- "Day Zero" investing — supports founders from inception, before ideas or teams exist
- Runs structured residencies in 30+ cities, 8–10 week programs
- Screens "tens of thousands of individuals annually" based on structured trait selection
- Actively matches co-founders, pressure-tests new teams before investing
- Two phases: residency → investment
- 2026 announcement: streamlined six-week "Inception Residency"

### Capital
- Typical initial: $100K–$250K, up to $500K in early rounds
- 2026: raised to $150K net per company (up from prior)
- Equity: 7–10% stake, varies by geography and deal structure
- Vehicles: SAFE, convertible notes, equity
- Growth fund: Antler Elevate — $3M–$10M, Pre-Series A through Series C

### Backers / scale
- Backed by Eduardo Saverin (Facebook co-founder)
- 30+ cities globally
- Positioning itself as "a potential future challenger to the likes of Y Combinator and Entrepreneur First"

## Criticism surface (load-bearing for BlackOps positioning)

### From Glassdoor / Medium / TechCrunch

1. **Staff quality gap** — "MBA-type people who know nothing about startups"; described as "a copycat of Entrepreneur First, but with far lower quality staff and founders"
2. **Unfriendly terms (historical)** — TechCrunch (2021): Antler's terms have been less than "founder-friendly"; CEO Magnus Grimeland acknowledged this was true "in the early days"; jurisdictional inequity (a founder in Vietnam gets less favorable terms than one in the US)
3. **Rapid team formation pressure** — founders forced to commit to co-founders by week 3–4 without adequate vetting; "suboptimal decision" making is the founder-reported outcome
4. **Domain expertise shallowness** — teams ended up building in industries where "nobody had any experience" (agriculture, construction, medtech); Antler's advice to "get those white coats in" arrived too late
5. **Execution-layer gap** — first-person founder account (Martijn Nanne, Amsterdam cohort): "Insufficient specialist recruitment… vague problem validation processes leading to late-stage pivots… limited support differentiating between solution-focused versus problem-focused ideas"
6. **Scale doubts** — "Questions remain about whether this model will work at scale and if it can generate superior returns over the long term"

### Direct quotes worth keeping

> "It is amazing to meet a group of 70 people that all left their jobs to start a startup. That is the primary thing that makes Antler unique." — Martijn Nanne (cohort participant, Amsterdam)

> "Antler's terms for startups have been less than 'founder-friendly'" — TechCrunch, 2021

> "I think in general we get very good feedback on our founder terms." — Magnus Grimeland (Antler CEO, rebuttal)
