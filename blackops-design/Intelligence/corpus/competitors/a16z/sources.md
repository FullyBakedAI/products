---

## Key findings

### Scale
- 1,076+ portfolio companies (Nov 2025)
- $46B AUM (July 2025), ranks first on US venture by AUM

### Execution-layer "platform" (the load-bearing comparable)
- Operates "more like a full-service advisory firm than a traditional VC"
- Built "an extensive operational platform" covering:
  - Talent recruitment
  - Marketing support
  - Business development
  - Technical guidance
- **New Media team** runs "launch as a service" — films launch videos, coordinates interviews, drafts essays, creates social media posts; positioned as faster than "typical branding agencies"; goal: portfolio companies "win the internet for a day"
- Launched 2026 fellowship program: 8 weeks training "operators, creators, and storytellers" to be deployed to portfolio companies for launches

### Implication for BlackOps
- The execution-layer gap BlackOps names is acknowledged at the top of the market — a16z has invested in in-house operators to fill it
- BlackOps's wedge must therefore be: (a) integrated/coherent vs. a16z's function-by-function platform, (b) accessible to non-a16z-backed founders (99%+ of the market), (c) tier-1 quality from day one, not training-fellowship output
- Risk: "a16z does this" is a frequent VC objection; BlackOps must have a clean answer
