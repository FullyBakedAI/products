# BO-PROBLEM-002 — Scattergun-Slither Failure Pattern

> Operator quote, recent case (2026-04-21, founder Discovery, Turn 3):
>
> *"I've watched one recently where they thought that they knew everything. They had a good idea, but the reality is that they didn't know how to create that idea, how to build momentum and how to drive adoption. So they ended up scattergunning a thin slither across a business rather than really nailing something that's compelling… when you don't do that it's very easy to just let things drift and end up with kind of tech debt and slop."*

## The pattern, decomposed

| Stage | What happens | Why it happens |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Over-confidence | Founder assumes they already know how to execute. | Strong technical/domain background is mistaken for full-stack execution capability. |
| 2. Thin spread | Effort is distributed across brand, product, marketing, GTM, org — each gets a sliver of the attention needed. | No model for sequencing; no discipline to go deep on one compelling thing before the next. |
| 3. Nothing lands | No single part is executed to a standard that actually compels a user, partner, or next-round investor. | A slither is not a cut. Depth is the signal; breadth without depth reads as *thin*. |
| 4. Drift | Without one compelling thing working, the team can't rally or prioritise. Momentum stalls. | Compelling execution is what *creates* momentum — without it, momentum has no source. |
| 5. Slop | Technical debt accumulates faster than value. The product becomes harder to change. | Nothing has a clean spine, so every addition complicates rather than extends. |

## Relationship to BO-PROBLEM-001

BO-PROBLEM-001 is the **macro / fund-level** view: the execution gap, Adoption Alpha, 75% failure.

BO-PROBLEM-002 is the **micro / company-level** view of the *same phenomenon from the inside*. It's what the fund-level failure rate looks like when you zoom into one portfolio company.

Together they frame BlackOps' value proposition at two altitudes:
- To the fund: "we prevent portfolio death at scale" (macro case — BO-PROBLEM-001)
- To the founder inside a portfolio company: "we prevent the scattergun-slither spiral" (micro case — this atom)

## BlackOps' counter-move (as stated by the operator)

BlackOps exists to **force compelling differentiation** — to pick the one thing, do it to a tier-1 standard, and let that carry the rest. See `BO-POSITION-001` for the positioning atom this problem motivates.

The operator's Fathom track record is positioned as evidence that this counter-move works: portfolio companies differentiated in the market (Axis Fintech, Pyram, OpenGamma, Mosaic Smart Data, BlockX, Talos, Capitec) rather than being thin-slither-distributed.

## Why this atom is load-bearing

If this pattern is real and common, BlackOps has a sharp, recognisable diagnostic:

- **To a VC:** *"Show me a portfolio company that's scattergunning a slither. We'll show you what compelling depth looks like on one cut, and you'll see the difference in 4 weeks."*
- **To a founder:** *"You think you know what to do. Let us prove that you're spreading thin across five things that all need to be deep on one. If we're wrong, no harm done; if we're right, we save your runway."*

If the pattern is not as common as the operator experiences it, BlackOps' wedge is dulled — and the pitch needs a different entry point.

## Tensions / self-criticism

- **Selection bias risk.** The operator has watched specific companies die scattergunning. We have not tested whether companies that scattergun but ship one compelling thing anyway are also in the sample. Without the counter-example set, the causal claim is weaker than the correlational one.
- **Founder psychology dimension under-specified.** "Thought they knew everything" is a psychology claim, not just a capability claim. BlackOps may need to package itself in a way that lets an over-confident founder accept help without losing face — a *sales* / *delivery* design problem, not just an execution problem.
- **Language transfer risk.** "Scattergun-slither" is vivid internal shorthand. For outside-facing material (pitch, site), it may need translating — e.g. "execution without a spine," "breadth without depth." Track.
